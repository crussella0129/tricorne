This is standard practice for upstream open source projects (Firefox /

You get broad rights to the code and artwork *content*. You do not get

provenance of what you ship.

## What you can do without asking

- Use the name "Tricorne" in unmodified factual reference: "I use
  Tricorne", "my talk is about Tricorne", "this tool was packaged in
  Tricorne".
- Link to the Tricorne project's official website and repositories
  using the project name.
- Reproduce the logo at display sizes in unmodified form for factual
  reference in articles, blog posts, talks, and educational material.
- Fork this repository and maintain your own downstream version — **as
  long as** you rename it and remove Tricorne branding before
  distributing binaries or ISOs to the public.
- Use the color palette and visual language (deep violet, firebrick
  cockade red, tricorne silhouette) as *inspiration* for your own
  distinct branding. Don't recreate the specific logo, wordmark, or
  lockups.

## What requires permission

- Using the "Tricorne" name or logo to market a product, service,
  course, distribution, or commercial offering.
- Distributing modified ISOs, RPMs, or other artifacts under the
  "Tricorne" name.
- Using the logo as a trademark of your own product.
- Creating merchandise bearing the Tricorne name or logo for sale.
- Registering a domain, social-media handle, or organization name that
  includes "Tricorne" and is substantially related to security,
  Linux, or Fedora.

For these uses, email `trademarks@thread-and-signal.com` (pre-v0.1:
`crussella0129@gmail.com` with `[TRICORNE TRADEMARK]` in the subject).
We are generally cooperative about community uses; we are strict about
uses that imply endorsement or could confuse users about provenance.

## Fedora-specific note

If and when Tricorne becomes an official **Fedora Spin**, branding
guidance may transfer in part to the Fedora Project's spin trademark
process. Until that happens, the terms above apply.

## Downstream forks

We welcome downstream forks, including commercial forks. Many
successful projects exist because of their fork ecosystems. The only
hard requirement is that forks distributed to the public **remove or
replace the Tricorne name and marks before distribution**, so users are
not misled about what they are getting.

The practical analog: CentOS → Rocky / Alma showed how to fork
gracefully. You may do the same.

## Questions

Trademark questions: email as above. Not every question has an obvious
answer; we would rather discuss early than surprise anyone with a
cease-and-desist letter. We have never issued one. We would like to
keep it that way.
